Beef Teriyaki Bowl

Description

Inspired by a Hello Fresh meal recipe, Beef Bulgogi Bowls, this recipe is a delicious, healthy, well-balanced meal great for lunch or dinner.

Ingredients

Makes 4 servings

  • 1 Scallion Bunch
  • 2 cucumbers
  • 8 oz shredded carrots
  • 1 cup Jasmine rice
  • 1 pound of ground beyond beef
  • 5 tsp white wine vinegar
  • 8 oz teriyaki sauce
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • 2 TBSP butter
  • 2 tsp vegetable oil
  • salt and pepper
  • Optional: Sour cream, Sesame seeds, Siracha

Steps

  1. Wash and dry all produce.
  2. In a small pot, combine rice, 2 cups of water, and a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil, then cover and reduce to low simmer. Cook until rice is tender, 12-18 minutes. Keep off heat until ready to serve.
  3. Meanwhile, trim and thinly slide scallion, separating whites from greens. Trim ends from cucumber. Using a peeler, shave cucumber lengthwise into ribbons, rotating as you go, until you get to the seedy core; discard core.
  4. In a medium bowl, combine half the vinegar, 1 tsp sugar, and a pinch of salt. Toss cucumber ribbons in bowl with vinegar mixture. Set aside, tossing occasionally, until ready to serve.
  5. Heat a drizzle of oil in a large, preferably nonstick, pan over medium high heat. Add carrots and cook, stirring, until just tender, 1 minute. Season with salt and pepper. Turn off heat; transfer to a plate.
  6. Heat another drizzle of oil in same pan over medium-high heat. Add scallion whites and cook until fragrant, 1 minute. Add beef and a pinch of salt and pepper. Cook, breaking up meat into pieces, until browned and cooked through, 4-6 minutes. Stir in remaining vinegar. Cook 30 seconds, then stir in teriyaki sauce. Bring to a simmer, then immediately turn off heat. Season again with salt and pepper.
  7. Fluff rice with a fork. Stir in 2 TBSP butter and season with salt and pepper; divide between bowls. Arrange beef, carrots, and pickled cucumber on top. (TIP: Drain any excess liquid from cucumber before adding.) Add sour cream, sesame seeds if desired. Garnish with scallion greens and as many remaining sesame seeds as you like.
